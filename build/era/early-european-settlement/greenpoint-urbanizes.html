Early European Settlement

Greenpoint Urbanizes

Urban development in Williamsburgh was initially focused on the Southside and soon after in the annexed quarter east of Union Avenue (“East Williamsburgh”). Further north, industrialist Neziah Bliss, who had married into the Meserole family, saw Greenpoint as a convenient place for shipbuilding and building housing for shipbuilders. Bliss, with his partner Eliphalet Nott (president of Union College upstate), laid out the Greenpoint street grid – then A through Q streets –in 1834.
