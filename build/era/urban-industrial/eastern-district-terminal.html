Eastern District Terminal

With industry dominating the Williamsburg waterfront, manufacturers needed a way to move raw goods and products in and out of the neighborhood. With the advent of the railroad the Hudson and East Rivers became barriers rather than the conduits they had been with the opening of the Erie Canal. National freight lines terminated on the Hudson shore of New Jersey and the only way across New York Harbor was to put freight cars onto barges and float them across the water. In the 1870s a rail freight terminal was established on the Northside waterfront to handle all these freight cars, expanding to become the Brooklyn Eastern District Terminal (BEDT) in 1906. A network of rails ran up and down Kent and Wythe avenues and many of the side streets, serving sugar refineries, breweries, barrel makers, and meat packers among many others. BEDT, operational until the 1980s, occupied the waterfront from North 6th to North 11th Street.

184 Kent Avenue, the former Austin Nichols & Co. building, once had lines for the railroad running through its ground floor. The southern portion of the former Brooklyn Eastern District Terminal is now The Edge, Northside Piers and Marsha P. Johnson East River State Park. The soccer field and the former CitiStorage building, which was only demolished in January, 2025, comprise the sections of Bushwick Inlet Park that were once part of the rail terminal.
