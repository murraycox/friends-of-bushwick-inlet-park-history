The History of Bushwick Inlet Park

Introduction to the Bushwick Inlet Park History

Bushwick Inlet Park is a 28-acre piece of land that sits along the East River at the juncture of Greenpoint and Williamsburg. The park was created in 2005, a giveback from the City of New York to the residents of North Brooklyn as part of a massive waterfront rezoning that so far has added more than 50,000 new residents to these neighborhoods. The design of the park is guided by a master plan design prepared by Donna Walcavage of Stantec and endorsed through a community process in 2007.

Bushwick Inlet Park

When it is completed, Bushwick Inlet Park will be the crown jewel set in the midst of 5 miles of almost continuous waterfront esplanades running from the Wallabout Creek to Newtown Creek. Set between Marsha P. Johnson East River State Park to the south and the Greenpoint Monitor Museum to the north, BIP and its immediate neighbors will provide close to 36 acres of contiguous public open space directly on the East River. But almost 20 years after the rezoning, the park is still more of a concept than a reality. As of 2023, less than a third of the park has been constructed, and it was only in the past few years that the City acquired – at great expense – the final piece of the puzzle that makes up Bushwick Inlet Park. Completion of the full park is likely decades away.

The Bushwick Inlet Park “puzzle” consists of 5 pieces of property, acquired at various times between 2007 and 2016 and scheduled to be developed in phases.

Motiva

At the north end of the park, wrapping the inlet itself on three sides is the 2-acre Motiva site, the only part of the park geographically within Greenpoint.

Bayside Oil

On the south side of the inlet is the 10.4-acre Bayside Oil site, whose large oil tanks were a stark reminder of the environmental degradation of waterfront until their demolition in 2019.

50 Kent Avenue

South of the Bayside property on Kent Avenue is a 2-acre, half-block parcel known as 50 Kent Avenue that opened in 2021 as the newest piece of green space to be added to the Park.

CitiStorage

To the west and south of 50 Kent is the former CitiStorage property, 8.5 acres in total, which includes the large blue and white building between North 10th and North 11th Streets. (That building is slated for demolition in 2024.)

North Brooklyn Parks headquarters and community house

The southernmost block of Bushwick Inlet Park is the 4.6-acre soccer field site, also home to the North Brooklyn Parks headquarters and community house. The soccer field was the first piece of Bushwick Inlet Park to open, in 2009.

The Bushwick Inlet Park History Project tells the story of Bushwick Inlet Park, Bushwick Creek and the surrounding neighborhoods of Williamsburg and Greenpoint through historic maps and photographs. The history is divided into five eras, covering pre-European contact through the present day and beyond. To navigate this story, start by clicking on one of the era buttons at the left of the screen (or top of the screen if you are viewing on a handheld device). You can also use the timeline at the bottom of the page, which will expand to show the chapters of each era.

Explore the history of the sites

This project uses many historic maps in addition to photographs and other resources to tell the history of Bushwick Creek and Bushwick Inlet Park. Historic maps were georeferenced to align with current-day geographies. Georeferencing is particularly tricky when it comes to shorelines, creeks and water features, which shift over time and may not have been as accurately mapped as more terrestrial features were.
